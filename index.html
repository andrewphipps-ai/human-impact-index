Methodology & Future Plans

Framework, data sources, and strategic direction for the Human Impact Index

Research Methodology

The Human Impact Index employs a multi-dimensional framework that prioritizes human welfare over purely economic metrics. Our momentum scores integrate quantitative employment data with qualitative assessments of skill transformation, regional concentration, and social impact.

Core Data Sources

  • UK Office for National Statistics (ONS) Labour Force Survey
  • US Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) Occupational Employment Statistics
  • Industry-specific employment databases and trade association reports
  • Academic research on automation susceptibility by occupation
  • Regional economic development data and workforce statistics

Momentum Score Components

  • Automation Velocity: Speed of technology deployment and adoption
  • Employment Exposure: Percentage of roles susceptible to automation
  • Skills Disruption: Degree of required workforce reskilling
  • Geographic Concentration: Regional employment clustering effects
  • Transition Pathways: Availability of alternative career routes

Future Development

Q2 2026: Launch of Europe and Asia-Pacific regional analyses

Q3 2026: Introduction of interactive scenario modeling tools

Q4 2026: Quarterly update cycle with real-time employment tracking

Research Philosophy

This index distinguishes itself from existing AI impact tools by centering human consequences rather than corporate efficiency gains. We examine not just job displacement numbers, but the lived experience of workers facing automation, the communities dependent on threatened industries, and the systemic inequalities that AI transformation may amplify or ameliorate.

"The question is not whether AI will transform work, but how we ensure that transformation serves human flourishing rather than merely economic optimization."

— Andrew Phipps, PhD Candidate in Philosophy, University of Sussex
